About the Decision Tool

How the Risk Assessment Works

This tool was designed to assess a stray, feral or any outdoor cat's potential risk to wildlife in south central Wisconsin. It primarily services Dane, Columbia, Dodge, Jefferson, Rock, Green, Lafayete, Iowa, and Sauk Counties. A cat's location in the environment is assessed by its distance from nearby sensitive wildlife areas. Wildlife areas are categorized into Low, Medium, or High sensitivity levels. When a cat's location is within 1200 meters of a highly sensitive or 400 meters of a medium sensitve wildlife area, the cat's location is assessed as a High Risk to wildlife. When a location is within 1200 meters of a medium sensitve or 400 meters of a low sensitive area, the cat's location is assessed as Medium Risk to wildlife. Every other location is assessed as a Low Risk to wildlife.

The areas shown in the tool include Wisconsin DNR managed areas (wildlife areas, conservation areas, state natural areas, state parks, etc), US Fish and Wildlife managed areas (wildlife refuges and waterfowl production areas), Nature Conservancy properties, Madison Audubon Society properties, The Prairie Enthusiast properties, Wisconsin County Parks, Madison City Parks, and private conservation easements.
**These areas are not entirely inclusive of all sensitive wildlife areas in south central Wisconsin. User discretion is needed to view the basemap for other potential sensitive wildlife areas.

Extra Data Layers

Cat Found Locations are locations that cats have been found by the public and eventually surrendered to the Dane County Humane Society. This is only a subset of the total data.

Important Bird Areas identify and recognize key sites that are critical to the protection of bird populations. These sites provide essential habitat to one or more species of breeding or non-breeding birds. They are designtated and assessed through a partnership of organziations including BirdLife International, the National Audubon Society, the WI DNR, and many more. Find out more at the Wisconsin Bird Conservation Partnership website.
